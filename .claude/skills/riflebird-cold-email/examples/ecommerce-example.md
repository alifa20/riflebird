# E-Commerce Cold Email Example
## Based on: One Six Eight London Micro-Audit

This example shows how to transform an e-commerce SEO audit into three cold email variations.

---

## Source Audit Summary

**Company**: One Six Eight London
**Domain**: onesixeightlondon.com.au
**Industry**: E-commerce (Designer Homewares)
**Location**: Cheltenham, VIC (Melbourne)
**Products**: Wall clocks, vases, mirrors, jewellery boxes

**Top 3 Issues Found**:
1. Title tag only 20 chars (should be 60) - missing products, location, USPs
2. 16 of 23 images missing alt text (70%) - invisible in Google Images
3. Meta description only 30 chars (should be 120-160) - not highlighting 5-year warranty, price match

**Positive Findings**:
- 5-year warranty (vs competitors' 1-year)
- Price match guarantee
- Melbourne showroom at 78 Herald St, Cheltenham
- "Designed in London" heritage
- Great product photography

**Competitive Landscape**:
- Temple & Webster (large player)
- Adairs (homeware chain)
- Strong USPs not leveraged

Most homewares stores rank for "designer wall clocks melbourne" because they use the full 60-character title. With your Cheltenham location and design heritage, you should own those searches.

Worth a 15-minute call to show you the exact fixes? Takes about 2-3 hours to implement in Shopify.

Best,
Ali Fathieh
Digital Marketing Specialist
Riflebird Agency
Melbourne

## Key Differences Between Variations

| Element | Variation A | Variation B | Variation C |

| **Tone** | Friendly, empathetic | Professional, data-driven | Bold, opportunity-focused |

| **P.S. Purpose** | Personal compliment | Highlight USP | Remove objection |

## Which Variation to Use When

### Variation A (Customer Perspective):
**Use When**:
- First contact (cold outreach)
- Consumer-focused brands
- Businesses that value customer experience
- Design/creative industries
- Building rapport is priority

**Avoid When**:
- Time-conscious executives
- Data-driven decision makers
- They receive lots of cold emails

---

### Variation B (Competitor Comparison):
**Use When**:
- Competitive markets
- Ambitious business owners
- They know their competitors
- Market positioning matters
- B2B or professional buyers

**Avoid When**:
- They're the market leader
- Non-competitive niche
- Collaborative industry culture
- They don't care about rankings

---

### Variation C (Benefit-First/Paid Ads):
**Use When**:
- They're running Google Ads
- Budget-conscious businesses
- Data-driven decision makers
- Want to introduce case study
- ROI is main concern

**Avoid When**:
- They don't run paid ads
- Already have good organic presence
- Not concerned about costs
- Prefer relationship-building approach

## A/B Testing Recommendations

### Test 1: Opening Hook
- Send Variation A to 50% of e-commerce prospects
- Send Variation C to 50% of e-commerce prospects
- Measure: Open rates, reply rates
- Hypothesis: Variation C's paid ads angle will resonate with ad-dependent businesses

### Test 2: Tone
- Variation A (friendly) vs Variation B (professional)
- Segment by company size (small = A, larger = B)
- Measure: Reply quality and conversion to calls

### Test 3: Case Study Inclusion
- With case study (Variation C) vs without (Variation A)
- Measure: Whether case study increases or decreases reply rates
- Theory: Some prospects want proof, others find it too salesy

## Common Customizations for E-Commerce

### Seasonal Timing:
Add to any variation:
```
P.S. Christmas shopping season starts in 8 weeks - ideal time to fix image SEO before the search traffic surge.
```

### Product-Specific:
For fashion/apparel:
```
70% of your outfit photos don't appear in Google Images - missing "winter outfit ideas" and "style inspiration" searches
```

For homewares:
```
Your styling and lifestyle shots are Instagram-worthy but invisible in Google Images where shoppers look for "[room] décor ideas"
```

For beauty/cosmetics:
```
Product images don't show in "how to use [product]" or "[product] before after" image searches
```

### Shopify-Specific:
```
All fixes can be done in Shopify admin - no developer needed. Title/meta in settings, alt text in product image editor.
```

### Free Shipping Threshold:
If they offer free shipping:
```
Your "Free AU shipping $99+" isn't in search results - competitors showing "Free Shipping" in their listings get 30% more clicks
```

**Subject**: Quick Shopify fix for your image search visibility

```

Sent you a note earlier this week about One Six Eight London's Google visibility.

Your 23 product images on the homepage don't have descriptions. Here's how to fix in Shopify:

1. Products → Select product → Images
2. Click "Add alt text" under each image
3. Format: "[Color] [product type] [size] [collection name]"
4. Example: "Black silent wall clock 40cm ELSA design"

Takes about 30 minutes for the homepage. Within 2-3 weeks, your products will start appearing in Google image searches for:
- "designer wall clocks"
- "modern vases"
- "luxury mirrors melbourne"

No strings attached - just seems like low-hanging fruit worth mentioning.

If you want the full breakdown of the title and meta description fixes too, just reply "send it" and I'll email them over.

Best,
Ali Fathieh
Riflebird Agency
Melbourne

P.S. Checked Temple & Webster - they have alt text on 100% of images. Worth matching them.
```

**Why This Follow-Up Works**:
- Provides immediate actionable value
- Specific instructions (not vague advice)
- Realistic time estimate (30 minutes)
- Includes expected results timeline
- Low-friction CTA ("send it")
- Builds trust through free help
- Competitive angle in P.S.

## Common Objections & How These Emails Handle Them

### "Too expensive":
- Addressed in Variation C P.S.: "Not trying to sell you a huge SEO retainer"
- Shows time estimate: "2-3 hours of work"
- Implies DIY option: "Shopify admin changes"

### "Too busy":
- 15-minute call commitment
- Specific time estimates for fixes
- Follow-up offers to "just send the fixes"

### "Don't understand SEO":
- Zero jargon throughout
- Visual metaphors (billboard, invisible products)
- Business impact language

### "Already working with someone":
- Not addressed directly (avoid mentioning it)
- Position as specific audit findings
- Free value approach builds goodwill

### "Not sure it will work":
- Variation C includes case study
- Specific outcome predictions (25-40% increase)
- Competitor examples show it's possible

## Template Adaptations for Other E-Commerce Niches

### Fashion/Apparel:
- Focus: Outfit images in Google Images, size/color variations missing
- Hook: "Your outfit photos don't show up in style inspiration searches"
- USP Example: "Free returns, Australian-made, sustainable materials"

### Electronics:
- Focus: Product specs in search results, comparison pages
- Hook: "Your products don't show pricing/specs in search results"
- USP Example: "Australian warranty, local support, price match"

### Beauty/Cosmetics:
- Focus: Ingredient listings, before/after images, tutorial content
- Hook: "Your before/after results don't appear in Google Images"
- USP Example: "Cruelty-free, Australian-made, dermatologist-tested"

### Food/Gourmet:
- Focus: Recipe images, ingredient sourcing, local availability
- Hook: "Your recipes don't show up in Google image searches"
- USP Example: "Organic certified, farm-direct, Melbourne delivery"

## Success Metrics for E-Commerce Cold Emails

Track these metrics to optimize:

1. **Open Rate**: Should be 45%+ (subject line quality)
2. **Reply Rate**: Should be 15%+ (email quality)
3. **Positive Reply Rate**: Should be 60%+ of replies (value provided)
4. **Call Booking Rate**: Should be 30%+ of positive replies
5. **Time to First Reply**: Faster = better (urgency/relevance)

**Red Flags**:
- Opens but no replies: Email body not compelling
- Replies but mostly "not interested": Wrong targeting or too salesy
- Lots of "send me the info": Good sign, opportunity to nurture
- No opens: Subject line needs work or wrong contacts

This example demonstrates how to transform a technical SEO audit into business-language cold emails that get responses from e-commerce prospects.
